The zigzag kiln.

The technology quietly changing Pakistan's brick industry — and the reason we rebuilt our kiln around it. Cleaner air in, stronger bricks out.

How it works

The fire never stops. It moves.

Inside the kiln, bricks are stacked so that they form distinct chambers. Openings between chambers are offset — so air can't run straight through. It is forced to travel a zigzag path, weaving chamber to chamber.

That longer journey changes everything: incoming air picks up heat from cooling bricks, reaches the firing zone already hot, burns the coal completely, then pre-heats the green bricks ahead — before leaving the chimney with far less smoke.
