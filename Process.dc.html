From earth to edifice.

Six stages stand between raw Punjab clay and a brick that rings like steel. None of them can be rushed. None of them are skipped.

مٹی سے عمارت تک
01

Stage 01 — The earth

Clay selection & tempering

Good bricks start under the ground. We dig the best local clay, test it, and temper it with water until it works like dough — the same fields this yard has trusted since 1983.

02

Stage 02 — The form

Molding — hand & machine

Two schools, one standard. Molded one at a time in wooden forms the way it began — or pressed by machine for perfect density and identical edges.

03

Stage 03 — The sun

Sun-drying in the open yard

Rows of raw bricks cure in open sun and wind, turned by hand for days until the moisture is exactly right. Rush this stage and the fire will find every flaw.

04

Stage 04 — The stack

Kiln setting

Dried bricks are stacked into chambers inside the kiln. The stacking itself is the engineering — it forms the zigzag path the fire will travel.

05

Stage 05 — The fire

Zigzag firing

Coal-fired heat snakes chamber to chamber, day and night. The zigzag draft burns hotter, cleaner and more evenly than any straight-line kiln — see how it works.

06

Stage 06 — The ear

Sorting & grading

Cooled bricks are struck, listened to, and graded by hand — Awal from Doem, by sound and eye. No raw brick leaves this yard.

Peak firing-zone temperature

980°C

Hot enough to vitrify clay into stone. The fire never goes out — it moves through the kiln, chamber by chamber, fed by hand around the clock.
